TOASTMASTERS INTERNATIONAL

Calicut Toastmasters Meeting #432 | Theme: **"A mother's touch, a world of love"**

Welcome to Table Topics!

What is Table Topics?

This is the segment dedicated to **impromptu speaking**. You will have **1 to 2 minutes** to deliver a mini-speech on a topic chosen for you. The goal is to organize your thoughts quickly, speak clearly, and deliver a coherent message.

Today's Session Brief

Our theme is **"A mother's touch, a world of love."** Each of the 10 topics is inspired by a visual **picture prompt** and is framed within the context of **Kerala's rich culture and family life**. Speak from the heart, connect the image to the question, and share your personal story!

1. The Comforting Embrace of the *Tharavad*

Introduction: This image of a warm embrace within a familiar setting reminds us of the anchor that is the Kerala home.

Question: Describe the overwhelming feeling of **solace or acceptance** you received from a mother figure immediately upon returning home to Kerala after a long time away (for work, studies, or travel). How did that "touch" ground you again?

2. The Enduring Legacy of Language and Values

Introduction: This scene captures the quiet transfer of culture, language, and moral fabric across generations.

Question: Recall a piece of **Malayalam wisdom** (a proverb, *pazhamchollu*, or a life lesson) you learned from your mother or grandmother. Explain the meaning of that wisdom and how that "touch" of guidance continues to shape your decisions today.

3. The Silent Sacrifice of the *Pathiri* and *Tiffin* Box

Introduction: The care poured into preparing food, especially for those leaving home, is a profound form of "A mother's touch."

Question: Describe the moment you truly realized the effort and **unseen sacrifice** that went into a specific meal (like a perfectly wrapped *pathiri* or *puttu*) or a packed lunch you received. How did that realization change your "world of love" and appreciation?

4. The Quiet Strength of the *Muthalali* (Matriarch)

Introduction: The Kerala mother often embodies quiet resilience, managing household and family affairs amidst economic challenges or changing times.

Question: Share a moment when your mother figure showed incredible **determination or resilience** (e.g., managing the household when a family member was abroad, or dealing with a crisis). How did her "touch" of unwavering spirit inspire your own confidence?

5. The Acceptance Beyond Expectation

Introduction: Unconditional love is the acceptance we need when we deviate from expected paths (career, life choices, etc.).

Question: Describe a time when your mother's love or support felt truly **unconditional**—specifically when you made a major life decision that was *not* the traditional or expected path (e.g., choosing an art career over engineering). How did that powerful "touch" of acceptance affirm your choices?

6. The Guiding Light of the *Nilavilakku*

Introduction: The lighting of the *Nilavilakku* (traditional oil lamp) is a daily act of consecration, representing hope and values.

Question: The lamp represents the light of tradition and prayer. Describe how a mother figure passed on a specific **spiritual value or ritual** to you, and how that "touch" of faith helps you maintain balance in your busy life in Calicut today.

7. The Taste of Love in the *Sadya*

Introduction: The *Sadya* (feast) is not just food; it is a labor of love, hospitality, and community spirit.

Question: Think about a time when your mother hosted a large family gathering (*Sadya*). Focus on a small detail of her preparations—a specific dish, a serving gesture, or her welcoming smile. How did that detail embody her **"world of love"** through hospitality?

8. The Comfort of the Monsoon Rains

Introduction: Kerala's monsoons are powerful. A mother's role is often to turn that potential chaos into an oasis of safety and warmth.

Question: Describe the atmosphere and sense of **security** created by a mother figure during a heavy Kerala monsoon rain. What specific "touch" (e.g., a hot drink, a story, or a blanket) turned the weather outside into a cozy "world of love" inside your home?

9. The Unspoken Stories in the Album

Introduction: Family photo albums are repositories of shared history, often narrated by the matriarch.

Question: Choose one photograph from your childhood (real or imagined) that represents a pivotal moment in your relationship with your mother. What **story or lesson** did she pass on about that moment, and how does her "touch" of memory keep that history alive?

10. The Encouragement for the Stage

Introduction: We often find our voice because someone believed in us first. This slide represents that initial source of confidence.

Question: Think about your journey to finding your voice (in Toastmasters or professionally). Describe the one **"touch" of initial encouragement**—a phrase, a look, or a specific piece of advice—your mother figure gave you that helped you overcome the fear of public speaking or standing on stage.
